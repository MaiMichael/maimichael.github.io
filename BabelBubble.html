Babel Bubble

A 2.5D crazy platformer game based on comics!

Goals and responsibilities

Create a game about "bubbles" with a team of 8 people for the Global Game Jam.

As the only designer my responsibilities were:

  • Ground the ideas produced during the ideation meetings by refining them and creating a game with a clear gameplay and objective.
  • Keep the scope of the project under control.
  • Responsible for the whole level design process, from mock up to prototype to final iteration with great focus on learning and difficulty curve suitable for a short game. Players learn how to play and master the mechanics in one level.
  • Maintained communication between artists and programmers to ensure cohesive remote work and fostered communication and collaboration.
  • Heavily tested the level to ensure maximum balance.
  • Created intro and ending cinematics for the game using Comic Book Cutscene - DarkDreamStudio.

What is Babel Bubble?

Babel Bubble is a cartoony comic style 2.5D platformer in which players control a rebellious superhero who tired of being pushed, pulled, and inked into endless battles and clich√©d storylines, break free from the constraints of narrative control. Armed with sturdy speech bubbles to use as platforms and explosive ones to beat up bad guys, the hero sets their sights on the ultimate escape: a towering urban style Babel's Tower that houses the portal to the real world.

Babel Bubble was developed for the Global Game Jam 2025 with a team of 8 people in 5 days.

Level Design process

My aim for this game was to create a short but smooth experience that could provide a sense of mastery and accomplishment to players. The level would develop around the outside of a tower that gets more and more crazy as the player climbs it (like the babel tower).

Each floor introduce a new mechanic and the player have to go around the tower practicing it.

  • The first floor introduces jump and later the sturdy bubble.
  • The second enemies and the spiky bubble.
  • The third introduces hard jumps and freefalls.
  • The fourth gives the illusion of change of the world prospective making the game harder.
  • The fifth and last requires mastery and precise jumps.

There's no game over in the game, failing a jump results in falling to floors beneath while being hit by an enemy will push the player far away .
